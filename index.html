Group Project

Project Idea is for a Smart Digital Alarm Clock that is Essentially an extension of a phone combined with a USB charger. Integrating several commonly available modules, a Raspberry Pi, Camera, Microphone, Speakers, 5VDC Power supply, Back-up Battery contactless charging pad, and Passive IR sensor to allow the user to perform all the roles that may need from an Alarm clock.

Functions:

General Alarm Clock (displays time and such),

Controllable via a network app or self-hosted website

Detects when the user gets out and stays out of bed to prevent them over sleeping and disables the alarm without user intervention.

Charges devices plugged into or resting on top of it.

Secure against all virtual intrusion

Integrates with external wearable sensors to wake the user during their optimal wake time. Giving the user a better waking experience.

Integrates with other home automation and Smart-home experiences. Such as Alexa, Google Home, Cortana, etc. Control lighting/blinds depending a schedule, user requests or other inputs.

Integrated Speakers for playing music.

External Display Support via HDMI allowing display on a TV or Monitor for Media PC or Chromecast like experience. Or Display can be used to show other information that may be requested but less efficient to give via sound such as news, weather, financial data, etc.

Recognise Official Alerts such as Bush fire warnings, evacuation orders, and similar to alert Users ASAP to such regardless of the time of day or their alert status.

Battery-Back-up for core functionality (not Charging devices) incase of power failure.

The Challenge for the group with this project would be writing and integrating all the software packages that are available to do all the functions but that may be written in different languages and with different intentions in mind. Given the availability of hardware relating to Raspberry PIs and the relative simplicity of this projects hardware we do not expect much of a problem there, unless the Pi proves insufficient to the processing needs of the software for the above functions which may either require us replacing it with a more powerful Single Board Computer or ditching some of the resource intensive functions.
